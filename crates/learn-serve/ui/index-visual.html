What are the three biggest mistakes people make about compound interest?
Three recur across this playlist. First, treating compound growth as linear — most viewers underestimate how dramatically a thirty-year horizon outperforms a twenty-year one, because the last decade is where exponential math finally bites [1]. Second, raiding the principal early; pulling out gains feels harmless but resets the curve [2]. Third, optimizing yield while ignoring fee drag — a 1% annual fee can eat roughly a quarter of a forty-year return [3].
